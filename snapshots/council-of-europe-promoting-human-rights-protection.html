Issue

Members of vulnerable groups across Europe continue to have their human rights eroded. A state that protects the human rights of all is less prone to instability and conflicts. This is of vital importance for all the UK’s partners worldwide and especially so in Europe.

Actions

We are:

  • reforming the European Court of Human Rights, implementing the Brighton Declaration adopted during the UK Chairmanship of the Committee of Ministers (November 2011 – May 2012)

  • supervising the execution of the Court’s judgment, holding governments to account for their Council of Europe (CoE) membership commitments

  • supporting CoE projects that assist member states combating lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender discrimination

  • increasing the use of CoE tools that help countries, including Georgia, Republic of Moldova, the Russian Federation, Armenia and Azerbaijan, meet their human rights commitments
