Our governance

The main decision-making, executive and managerial bodies at the National Measurement and Regulation Office.

NMRO Steering Board

The role of the steering board is to provide formal strategic planning, priorities, objectives and targets and new ways of working.

The board’s responsibilities include:

  • advising on the agency’s strategy
  • setting out its allocation of resources
  • agreeing business plans and monitoring the agency’s performance
  • highlighting risks and issues that could threaten the agency’s strategy or business plans
  • establishing agency capability and plans for the future

Members

Members of this board are:

  • Amanda Brooks
  • Peter Cowley

NMRO Management Board

The management board provides corporate leadership and makes day-to-day decisions about running NMRO. The board also produces the agency’s long-term business strategy to meet the needs of the stakeholders.

The board’s responsibilities include:

  • processes and resources relating to people and planning
  • financial management
  • communication
  • project management
  • IT
  • property
  • setting standards, values and controls within the agency

Members

NMRO Audit Committee

The audit committee advises the NMRO steering board. Its main function is to ensure accountability for public funds by improving and encouraging financial reporting and discipline.

The committee’s role is to support the chief executive in his role as accounting officer by:

  • overseeing the work of the internal and external auditors
  • making recommendations to the steering board and reviewing the annual financial statements before submission to the steering board

Members

Members of the audit committee are:

  • Alan Proctor, Chair
  • Peter Cowley, non-executive committee member
